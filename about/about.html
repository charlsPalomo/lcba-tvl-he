The Founder

Pedro Evangelista Llamas was born in Pagsanjan, Laguna on August 01, 1884. He studied in St. Joseph Catholic School in Hong Kong and earned his Bachelor of Science in Pharmaceutical Chemistry from the University of Michigan.

At 32, he served as Municipal President of Pagsanjan (1916–1920). In 1923, he founded the Pagsanjan Academy, and in 1930, established the Laguna Institute in Calamba — the institution that would become LCBA.

Don Pedro Llamas died January 13, 1946, leaving behind a legacy of humble servitude and a vision of equipping communities through quality education.

Philosophy, Vision & Mission

Philosophy

Inspired by Dr. Jose Rizal's educational principles, LCBA Education upholds a balanced curriculum towards holistic development for a better quality of life.

Vision

LCBA envisions to become an autonomous higher education institution committed to producing holistically developed and globally competitive lifelong learners.

Mission

LCBA commits itself to the total human development of the learners through value-laden and competency-based education, policy-relevant research, and sustainable community involvement.
